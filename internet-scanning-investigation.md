# Investigation: Internet-Wide Scanning Activity (Zmap / Stretchoid)

## Overview

During routine monitoring of network telemetry within the SIEM platform, multiple alerts were observed indicating automated scanning activity targeting internal hosts. 
The alerts were generated by the Suricata intrusion detection system and suggested that external sources were attempting to identify accessible services and application 
endpoints.

Further investigation revealed that the activity appeared to be associated with infrastructure linked to an organization known as stretchoid.com, which claims to conduct 
internet-wide scanning for research purposes.

## Monitoring Environment

Network telemetry was analyzed using the following tools:

- Elastic Stack (SIEM)
- Kibana dashboards for alert visualization
- Suricata IDS for intrusion detection

Threat intelligence sources used during the investigation included:

- VirusTotal
- GreyNoise
- AbuseIPDB
- Talos Intelligence
- WHOIS lookup services

## Detection

While reviewing Suricata alerts within the Kibana dashboard, the following IDS signatures were triggered:

- ET INFO Spring Boot Actuator Health Check Request
- ET SCAN Zmap User-Agent (Inbound)

These alerts suggested that an automated scanner was attempting to interact with exposed application endpoints.

The Zmap user-agent signature is commonly associated with internet-wide scanning tools used to enumerate services across large numbers of systems.

## Investigation Process

1. Identified the source IP address generating the alerts within the SIEM dashboard.
2. Reviewed traffic patterns to determine whether multiple connection attempts were occurring.
3. Investigated IP reputation using threat intelligence platforms.
4. Conducted infrastructure analysis to determine whether the IP addresses were related to a common organization.
5. Researched the domain associated with the scanning infrastructure.

## Observed Source Infrastructure

Multiple source IP addresses were identified during the investigation:

- 198.199.117.121
- 162.243.136.76
- 162.243.136.79

Each source generated similar scanning activity targeting internal services.

Threat intelligence platforms indicated that these hosts had been reported multiple times and were associated with scanning activity observed across the internet.

Further analysis revealed that the infrastructure appeared to be associated with a domain identified as **stretchoid.com**.

## Threat Intelligence Findings

Reputation analysis indicated that the identified IP addresses had multiple reports across threat intelligence platforms, suggesting previous observations of similar activity.

Research into stretchoid.com indicated that the organization claims to conduct internet-wide scanning for research purposes. However, the website provided minimal 
transparency regarding how collected data is used and required the submission of sensitive information in order to opt out of scanning.

Due to the lack of transparency and the volume of scanning activity observed, the legitimacy of the scanning activity could not be fully verified.

## Analysis

The observed behavior is consistent with automated reconnaissance scanning activity. Tools such as Zmap are frequently used to rapidly scan large portions of the internet 
in order to identify exposed services or vulnerable systems.

While some organizations perform internet scanning for legitimate research purposes, similar scanning techniques are also commonly used by threat actors to identify 
potential targets for exploitation.

The presence of multiple scanning hosts targeting the same environment suggests coordinated automated reconnaissance activity.

## Conclusion

Based on the IDS signatures, infrastructure correlation, and threat intelligence analysis, the activity was assessed to be automated internet scanning activity associated 
with infrastructure linked to stretchoid.com.

Although the scanning activity may be related to research efforts, the lack of transparency and repeated connection attempts warranted documentation and escalation.

## Response

The activity was documented and submitted as an incident investigation ticket for review by monitoring personnel.

Continued monitoring was recommended to determine whether the scanning activity persisted or escalated into exploitation attempts.
